About me
========

I am Thomas Perrot, an embedded software engineer based in Toulouse, France.
My work centres on the Linux kernel, the `Yocto Project`_ / `OpenEmbedded`_
ecosystem, `Zephyr RTOS`_, and open-source hardware. I contribute patches to
upstream projects and occasionally speak at events such as `Capitole du Libre`_.

You can find a full list of my open-source contributions on the
`Contributions`_ page, download my `Curriculum vitae`_, or visit my
`LinkedIn`_ profile.

About this blog
===============

This blog is a place where I write about topics I find interesting:

* **Embedded Linux** — Yocto Project releases, toolchain notes, board bring-up.
* **Zephyr RTOS** — device drivers, Bluetooth, build system.
* **Open-source hardware** — single-board computers, FPGA, retro-computing.
* **Linux gaming** — native ports, Proton, SteamOS, emulation.
* **Retro gaming** — hardware preservation, game engines, homebrew.
* **Competitive fitness** — CrossFit, Hyrox, powerlifting.

Long-form articles appear under `Articles`_. The weekly `Digests`_ are short
link roundups covering the same topics.
