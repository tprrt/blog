Retrogaming Digest — Week 23, 2026

Retrogaming

The retrogaming scene is thriving as developers and enthusiasts continue to breathe new life into classic games and consoles. Recent news highlights the emergence of homebrew games, nostalgic re-releases, and exciting new adaptations that keep the spirit of retro gaming alive.

New Releases and Homebrew Games

The revival of classic games through homebrew adaptations has taken center stage, showcasing the passion of independent developers.

  • An adaptation of Sega’s classic arcade game Head-On has been released for the Sinclair ZX Spectrum by Indigobeetle on Indie Retro News.
  • Studio MDHR announced a new 8-bit spin-off of Cuphead for the Sega Master System during Summer Game Fest 2026.

Celebrating Classic Titles

As the gaming community reminisces about the past, several platforms have begun to explore and document the history behind iconic games and their creators.

  • Wizardry’s co-creator shared insights about the transformative impact of the RPG in a video commemorating its 45th anniversary.
  • Retro Dodo featured a discussion on the best Pokémon games as voted by 10,000 retro gamers.

Re-releases and Retro Events

The continuous interest in classic titles is reflected in a series of re-releases and retrospectives that celebrate gaming history.

  • Retronauts provided a roundup of retro re-releases for the week, highlighting titles like Lanky Kong returning to modern platforms.
  • In episode 771 of the Retronauts podcast, the discussion centered on the beloved Asterix franchise, exploring its cultural significance.

In summary, the retrogaming landscape remains vibrant, enriched by new releases and nostalgic reflections on the classics that shaped the industry.
